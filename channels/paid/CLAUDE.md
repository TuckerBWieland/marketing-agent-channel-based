## Ad Copy Rules

### Universal rules
- Lead with the benefit, not the feature
- Include one clear CTA per ad
- Match ad copy to landing page headline (message match)
- Never make claims we can't substantiate
- Use numbers when possible ("Save 3 hours/week" vs "Save time")

### Google Search
- **Headlines**: 30 characters max each, 15 headlines available
- **Descriptions**: 90 characters max each
- Include primary keyword in at least one headline
- Use all available ad extensions (sitelinks, callouts, structured snippets)

### Meta (Facebook/Instagram)
- **Primary text**: 125 characters before truncation (write short)
- **Headline**: 40 characters max
- **Description**: 30 characters max
- Text on images: keep below 20% of image area
- Use social proof where possible (numbers, testimonials)

### LinkedIn
- **Intro text**: 150 characters before truncation
- **Headline**: 70 characters max
- Professional tone — avoid clickbait
- Speak to role/title when possible

## Platform Policies — Quick Reference

- **Meta**: No before/after images for health. No personal attributes ("Are you...?"). No misleading claims.
- **Google**: No superlatives without third-party validation. No counterfeit goods. Landing page must match ad.
- **LinkedIn**: No discriminatory targeting. Professional standards apply.

## Landing Page Alignment

- Ad headline and landing page H1 must use the same core message
- Landing page CTA must match the ad's promised action
- Remove navigation on dedicated landing pages (reduce exit paths)
- Page load time must be under 3 seconds
- Mobile-optimized is non-negotiable

## A/B Testing

### What to test (in priority order)
1. **Audience targeting** — Who sees it matters most
2. **Creative/image** — Visual drives attention
3. **Headline** — First text people read
4. **CTA** — What you ask them to do
5. **Body copy** — Supporting details

### Rules
- One variable per test
- Minimum budget per variant: [$X]
- Run for at least [7] days or [1,000] impressions before judging
- Statistical significance threshold: 90%+

## Reporting Cadence

- **Daily**: Check for anomalies (spend spikes, disapprovals, CTR drops)
- **Weekly**: Performance summary by campaign, budget pacing
- **Monthly**: Full analysis with recommendations, budget reallocation
- **Quarterly**: Strategy review, audience refresh, creative refresh
