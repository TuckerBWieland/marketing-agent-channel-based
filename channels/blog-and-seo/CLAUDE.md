## SEO Guidelines

### Keyword targeting
- Every article targets **one primary keyword** and **2-3 secondary keywords**
- Primary keyword appears in: title, H1, first 100 words, meta description, URL slug
- Secondary keywords appear naturally in H2s and body
- **Never keyword-stuff** — write for humans first

### Meta descriptions
- Length: 150-160 characters
- Include primary keyword
- Include a value proposition or hook

### URL slugs
- Format: `/blog/[keyword-slug]` — lowercase, hyphenated
- Keep under 60 characters

### Image SEO
- Every image gets descriptive alt text
- File names should be descriptive: `marketing-funnel-diagram.png`
- Compress to under 200KB where possible

## Article Structures

### Standard article (1,000-1,500 words)
```
# [Primary Keyword in Title]

Introduction (100-150 words) — hook, state what reader will learn

## [Section with secondary keyword]
## [Section]
## [Section]

## Key Takeaways
- Summary + CTA
```

### Listicle (1,200-2,000 words)
```
# [Number] [Ways/Tips] to [Achieve Outcome]

Introduction (100 words)

## 1. [Item] — 2-3 paragraphs each
## 2. [Item]
...

## Conclusion + CTA
```

### Pillar page (2,500+ words)
```
# The Complete Guide to [Topic]

Table of contents (linked)

## What is [Topic]?
## Why [Topic] Matters
## How to [Topic]: Step by Step
## Common Mistakes
## Tools & Resources
## FAQ
```

## Internal Linking

- Every article links to **2-3 related articles** on our blog
- Every article links to **1 relevant product page** where natural
- Use descriptive anchor text (not "click here")
- Pillar pages link to all cluster articles and vice versa
- When publishing new content, add links FROM 2-3 existing articles

## Content Refresh Policy

- **Review cadence**: All posts reviewed every [6/12] months
- **Update triggers**: Traffic decline >20%, outdated stats, product changes
- **When refreshing**: Update publish date, note substantive changes
- **Archive**: Redirect (301) irrelevant posts to best alternative

## CTA Placement

- **Inline**: One contextual CTA in the body (after most relevant section)
- **End**: Every article ends with a CTA (newsletter, related content, or product)
- CTAs should feel like a natural next step, not an interruption

## Publishing Workflow

1. **Draft** — Follow structure template
2. **SEO check** — Keyword placement, meta description, internal links
3. **Edit** — Voice, accuracy, clarity
4. **Approve** — [Who approves]
5. **Publish** — CMS, verify formatting, submit to Search Console
6. **Distribute** — Share via social, email, internal channels
